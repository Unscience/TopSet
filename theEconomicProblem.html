The problem of scarcity

Finite resources

Every country has resources, however, there is a limited number available

Limited resources are scarce

These resources are separated in groups known as the 4 factors of production

  • Land
  • Labor
  • Capital
  • Enterprise

Unlimited wants

Needs are the basic requirements for human survival; without them, humans would cease to exist

Wants are human desires, they are unlimited or infinite

The Economic Problem

This occurs because although our wants are infinite our resources (used to produce them) are limited

Therefore we have to choose how to allocate our scarce resources between different uses

There are 3 main questions we ask when choosing how allocating resources

  • What to produce
  • How to produce
  • For whom to produce for

what to produce

it is important for a country choose as they cannot produce everyone's wants

How to produce

The 4 factors of production can be organised differently, even when producing the same product

For whom to produce for

Goods have to be shared amongst the population but in what way. Ultimately it is 'who gets what'

Opportunity Cost

Once a choice has been made, a sacrifice has also been made. This is called the opportunity cost

Opportunity cost is the cost of the next best alternative given up when making a choice

Causes of Positive and Negative Economic Growth

Positive Economic growth

Here are some factors that may impact economic growth

  • New Technology
  • Improved Efficiency
  • Education and Training
  • New Resources

New Technology

This increases speed and reliability

Improved Efficiency

e.g. Kaizen (continuous improvement) and lean production (using fewer resorces in production)

means more resources are used up, instead of wasted

Education and Training

An economy's productivity increases as the proportion of eduacated workers increases

More educated workers can complete more complicated tasks (usually involving analysis,evaluation and critical thinking)

New Resources

e.g. population increase

more resources more tasks can be done and more products can be produced

The PPC curve shifts outward to show economic growth

At the new curve, you are able to produce more butter and guns

Negavuve economic growth

Negative economic growth can be caused by:

  • Resorce depletion
    • A lack of natural resources

  • Emigration for skilled workers
    • When skilled workers leave a country it reduces the productivity of the economy

  • Wars/conflicts and natural disasters
    • conflict may destroy major airport or drought may affect crop yeild

This causes the PPC to shift inwards showing a decrease in the maximum possible output
