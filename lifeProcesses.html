Enzymes

Enzymess are simply biological catalyststest. This means they speed up the rates of metabolic reactions (reaction which ocour in cells) just like any other catalysts. Like catalysts they remain unchanged after the reaction and is therefore able to catalyse more reactions.

The production of enzymes are dependent on the the coding genes in the DNA. Genes are codes for proteins, some of which are enzymes.

We require enzymes as metabolic reactions cannot occour at out body temperature. The enzymes ensure we can do so, even at our low temperature

How does it work

When an enzyme catalyses a reaction, it acts on a substratetest (the molecule in the metabolic reaction getting catalyst). Enzymes have a small area called an active sitetest which the substrated attaches to. Once the reaction takes place the products leave the active site. The enzyme is now free to catalyse other reactions

Lock and key model

the lock and key modeltest states a particular substate fits into a particular active site. This is why enzymes are spefic as they can only catalyse a substance with complememtry shape

Temperature

The optimum temperature is the temperature where the enzymes work the best. For humans it is at 37° (body temperature)

Higher temperatures will increase the rate of reaction as the molecules (enzymes and substrates) have more kinetic energy. This causes more frequent collisions and thus more sucessful collisions per second

However if the temperature is too high, the enzymes begin to denature test. This is caused as enzymes are proteins and proteins lose their shape when heated. The change in shape means that the active site is no longer complemetry to the sunstrate and thus cannot catalyse the reaction.

The process of denaturing is irreversable.

pH

the optimum pH similar to temperature is the pH where the enzyme works best. If the pH is too alkaline or acidic the enzyme will become denatured.

most human enzymes work at pH 7 however some enzymes like lipase (which acts in the stomach) has an optimum pH of 2 (around the same os the pH of the stomach acid)

Active transport test is the movement of molecules and ions against the concentration gradient (from a low concentration to a high concentration) using energy from respiration.
